Recipe: Pan-seared Steak

Description

As my personal favourite, steak is a phenomenal dish for special occasions. Luckily, it is a surprisingly simple dish. Steaks are meat generally sliced across the muscle fibers, potentially including a bone. It is normally grilled, though can also be pan-fried. To sear with a frying pan, make sure to use one with high thermal conductivity, such as cast-iron, stainless steel, or aluminium. Enjoy with potatoes, salad and your favourite drink for a satisfying dinner!

Ingredients

  • Steak Cut of your choice (Sirloin, Scotch Fillet, Ribeye, Porterhouse etc)
  • butter
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Canola Oil (high smoke point oil)
  • Garlic cloves (with skin)
  • Rosemary spriggs and leaves

Steps

  1. Prepare your steak cuts, making sure the meat surfaces have minimal moisture, and is generously seasoned
  2. Heat a frying pan on high heat, making sure it reaches temperature
  3. Oil your frying pan
  4. Lay the steak away from you, allowing it to sear. (Please refrain from touching the steak too much)
  5. After one minute, flip the steak, repeating the searing process for the other side
  6. Continue flipping in intervals until just below desired doneness
  7. Immediately reduce heat to low, or remove pan from the heat
  8. Place butter, crushed garlic cloves and rosemary spriggs onto the pan, basting the steak
  9. Once basted, remove steak from the frying pan and onto a plate/cutting board
  10. Let the meat rest for at least 10 minutes
  11. Serve with your choice of side dishes
